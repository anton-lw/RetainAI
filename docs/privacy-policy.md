# Privacy Policy

## Purpose

This Privacy Policy describes the information RetainAI is designed to process and the privacy controls included in the software. It is written as a template policy for adopters and stewards. Deploying organizations should adapt it to their legal entity, jurisdiction, and operating context.

## What RetainAI Does

RetainAI is software used by implementing organizations to support retention operations in development programs. It helps authorized staff review which beneficiaries may need follow-up, record actions taken, and evaluate whether those actions improved retention.

## Information Processed

Depending on how an organization configures RetainAI, the platform may process:

- beneficiary identifiers
- enrollment information
- demographic data
- geographic or site data
- program participation history
- attendance, appointment, or collection records
- outreach and intervention history
- free-text field notes
- consent and governance records
- system access logs and audit logs

## Why Information Is Processed

RetainAI is intended to support:

- retention monitoring
- follow-up prioritization
- intervention documentation
- program analytics
- model validation and quality review
- governance and audit requirements

It is not intended to make final automated decisions about eligibility or exclusion.

## Legal and Ethical Basis

The lawful basis for processing is determined by the deploying organization. The software supports multiple governance patterns, including consent tracking and opt-out from predictive modeling, but it does not by itself determine whether a deployment is lawful.

Deploying organizations are responsible for:

- identifying the lawful basis
- completing required notices and agreements
- validating compliance with local law

## Access and Disclosure

RetainAI is designed to limit access through:

- role-based permissions
- session controls
- audit logging
- export restrictions
- pseudonymized export defaults

Information should only be shared with authorized staff or legally authorized recipients.

## Data Retention

Retention periods are deployment-specific. Organizations using RetainAI should define:

- how long beneficiary records remain in the system
- how long logs and evaluation reports are retained
- how backups are retained
- when records are archived or deleted

## Security Measures

The repository includes:

- authentication and authorization
- secret handling and rotation support
- audit logging
- tokenization support
- encrypted transport expectations
- infrastructure guidance for secure self-hosting

No software can eliminate all risk. Organizations remain responsible for secure deployment, network controls, backup security, and operational governance.

## Beneficiary Rights and Requests

RetainAI includes support for:

- consent state recording
- explanation workflows
- opt-out from modeling

Organizations should define how they will handle access, correction, deletion, objection, or other applicable rights requests under local law.

## Cross-Border Data Handling

RetainAI includes residency-aware policy settings, but deploying organizations must ensure that infrastructure and transfer practices comply with applicable law and organizational policy.

## Contact and Stewardship

This repository is designed for open-source stewardship. The exact contact and controller identity will depend on the entity operating a deployment or stewarding the public repository. Adopters should publish their own controller/operator contact details alongside this template.
