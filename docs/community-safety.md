# Community Safety and Harmful Content Handling

## Why This Document Exists

RetainAI is not a social platform, but it does process user-entered text such as field notes, intervention notes, and governance notes. In a project serving vulnerable populations, that still creates safety obligations.

This document addresses:

- inappropriate or illegal content handling
- harassment prevention
- sensitive-user protections

## Product Context

RetainAI is designed for authorized staff use. It is not intended for open public posting or beneficiary-to-beneficiary interaction.

Typical user-generated content includes:

- case notes
- field observations
- intervention notes
- explanation notes
- governance annotations

## Prohibited Content

RetainAI deployments should prohibit storage or sharing of:

- threats or harassment
- hate speech or discriminatory abuse
- sexual exploitation content
- child sexual abuse material
- unlawful surveillance material
- unnecessary or gratuitous sensitive details
- content that could put a beneficiary at risk if exposed without operational need

## Required Operational Response

Deploying organizations should define a response path that includes:

1. immediate restriction of access where necessary
2. preservation of audit evidence
3. redaction or removal when lawful and appropriate
4. escalation to safeguarding, legal, or security leads
5. mandatory reporting where required by law

## Harassment and Unsafe Conduct

Contributor-community expectations are governed by [../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

For product users, operators should prohibit:

- abusive notes about beneficiaries or colleagues
- coercive or discriminatory operational behavior
- unauthorized surveillance or retaliatory use of the system

## Child and Other Sensitive Populations

Some deployments may involve children, displaced populations, patients, or other high-risk groups. In those contexts, operators should:

- minimize collected fields
- limit free-text content where practical
- train staff on note-writing discipline
- validate consent and explanation processes
- restrict exports aggressively

## Built-In Safety-Relevant Features

The repository includes:

- role controls
- audit logs
- consent and opt-out tracking
- beneficiary explanation support
- pseudonymized exports by default
- governance alerts

These support safer use, but they do not replace organizational safeguarding policy.

## Reporting Concerns

Safety concerns should be raised through the deployment operator or project steward using the most private available channel. Public disclosure is not appropriate when it could expose beneficiaries or ongoing incidents.
